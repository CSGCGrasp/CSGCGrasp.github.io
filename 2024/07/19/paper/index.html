2024-07-19

Comparison

Comparison between common strategy in semi-supervised learning and our semi-supervised method. Our semi-supervised framework uses a curriculum-scheduled augmentation to progressively increase input perturbation and a geometric consistency strategy to dynamically adjust thresholds and align perceptive and grasping locations, facilitating stable convergence and perception-grasping consistency.

Overview of our CSGC framework

An overview of our CSGC framework. Firstly, curriculum-scheduled augmentation progressively selects various augmentation operations to increase the input perturbation of the student as training progresses. Then, the teacher predicts score maps of segmentation and grasping detection. Sequentially, the geometric consistency regularization part utilizes the region-consistent pseudo-label filtering (RCPF) to produce pseudo-labels and the overall optimization objective to train parameter of the student. Finally, the parameter of teacher is updated by the exponential moving averaging (EMA).
